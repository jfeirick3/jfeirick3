Guide for My Yahtzee Game

Home Page   Favorites   My Game!  Guide for my game!

Yahtzee is a game that requires equal parts luck and skill

Pre-Game

When you start Yahtzee, you will have to enter some information.

The first thing you need to do is decide how many people you are playing the game with.

    The rules for player count are as follows:
  • There must be at least one player.
  • There cannot be more than 4 players.
  • Any invalid response, such as a number less than 1 or greater than 4, or a response that isn't a number will result in the player count automatically being set to 2.

After deciding how many players there will be, you must enter names for each player.

    The rules for naming are as follows:
  • The name must be at least 1 characters in length.
  • The name cannot be longer than 10 characters.
  • Your name may not comtain a comma.

After you give a name to all players, you must decide which mode to play on. The differences between game modes are explained at the bottom of the page.

How to Play

The person with the most points at the end of 13 rounds wins the game!

Points can be obtained by filling the requirements for one of thirteen boxes. Each box can only be used once.

Upper row

The entire upper row of boxes follows the same set of rules.

The score you gain is equal to the sum of your dice faces that have the specified number. For example:

If the player scored using the 'Six' box, they would earn 6 + 6 points.

Lower row

Three and Four Of a Kind: The points you earn is equal to the sum of all of your dice. Three/Four of these dice must have the same number of pips.

Full House: You must have 3 of one number and 2 of another. Score: 25

Small and Large Straight: A straight is a sequence of consecutive die faces; a small straight is 4 consecutive faces, and a large straight is 5 consecutive faces. The dice do not need to be in any specific orientation. Score: 30 (small straight) or 40 (large straight)

Chance: The score you earn is the sum of your dice.

Yahtzee: A 'Yahtzee' in this game is a five-of-a-kind. Score: 50

Different Game Modes

There are currently three modes: Normal, Competitive, and Super Competitive.

Scoring is the same no matter what gamemode you play on.

In Competitive mode, after a player has finished their turn, they choose to block one of the remaining boxes for the next player

When a box is blocked, a player cannot score using that box for that turn.

In Super Competitive mode, the effect is different. Selecting a box forces the next player to score using that box.
